Martin's Tank Care Tips

  • Cycle the Tank Before Adding Fish
    • Cycling a tank means to take the steps necessary to bring the water conditions up to where they are healthy for the fish
    • Never attempt to cycle your tank with fish already in it

  • Test and Monitor Water Parameters
    • Ammonia, nitrates, and nitrites are fine at low levels but left unchecked they can build up to unhealthy amounts
    • Test the pH of your water, if the water is very hard bring the pH down with chemicals

  • Perform Regular Water Changes
    • Changing water frequently is better than adding chemicals
    • Remove about one-third of the water from your tank every week and replace it with fresh, clean water

  • Research Fish Before Purchasing
    • It’s a beginner’s mistake to go to the pet store and purchase fish by looks alone
    • Do not overstock your tank

  • Avoid Overfeeding Your Fish
    • It is very difficult to starve a fish; one feeding per day is plenty
    • Do not feed more than the fish will eat in a few minutes

  • Manage Light in and Around Your Aquarium
    • Too much light will result in uncontrolled algae growth
    • You should keep the tank light on a maximum of 12 hours every day, but less is better
